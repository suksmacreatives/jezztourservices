How to Get Around Bali: Private Driver vs Scooter vs Taxi

Jezz Tour Service Feb 28, 2026

Overview

Bali is one of the most visited islands in Southeast Asia, attracting millions of travelers every year. However, unlike many big cities around the world, Bali does not have a comprehensive public transportation system. This means that choosing the right way to get around the island is essential to ensure a smooth and enjoyable travel experience.

Whether you are planning to explore beaches, cultural attractions, or hidden natural spots, transportation plays a major role in how much you can experience. In this guide, we will compare the three most popular transportation options in Bali: private drivers, scooter rentals, and taxis or ride-hailing services.

1. Private Driver – The Most Comfortable Option

Hiring a private driver is by far the most popular choice among international tourists visiting Bali. This option provides convenience, safety, and flexibility, making it ideal for families, couples, and first-time visitors.

Advantages:

  • Flexible schedule based on your itinerary
  • Comfortable air-conditioned vehicle
  • Local driver with knowledge about destinations
  • No need to worry about navigation or parking

Private drivers usually offer full-day services (8–10 hours), allowing you to visit multiple destinations in one day efficiently. This option is especially recommended if you want to explore areas like Ubud, Kintamani, or East Bali.

2. Scooter Rental – Flexible but Risky

Renting a scooter is a common choice for solo travelers or those staying in tourist areas such as Canggu, Seminyak, or Ubud. It provides maximum flexibility and is relatively inexpensive.

Advantages:

  • Affordable daily rental cost
  • Easy to navigate through traffic
  • Convenient for short-distance travel

Things to Consider:

  • Requires an international driving license
  • Traffic can be chaotic for beginners
  • Higher risk of accidents if inexperienced

While scooters offer freedom, they are not recommended for long-distance travel or for those unfamiliar with Bali’s road conditions.

3. Taxi & Ride-Hailing Apps

Taxis and online ride services like Grab or Gojek are widely available in Bali, especially in popular tourist areas. They are suitable for short trips and quick transportation needs.

Advantages:

  • Easy to book via mobile apps
  • Transparent pricing
  • Good for short distances

Limitations:

  • Limited availability in remote areas
  • Not ideal for full-day tours
  • Costs can add up for multiple trips

Which Option is Best?

The best transportation option depends on your travel style. If you value comfort and want to explore multiple destinations in one day, a private driver is the best choice. For short distances and flexibility, scooters and taxis can be useful alternatives.

Conclusion

Getting around Bali requires proper planning, as transportation options are different from major cities. By choosing the right method based on your needs, you can maximize your travel experience and explore the island efficiently. For most travelers, hiring a private driver offers the best balance of comfort, safety, and convenience.
